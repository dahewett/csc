The person who reported it is a fisher (Clallum County MRC member- Joe Schmitt) and he definitely observed them with mackerel in their mouth. But no pictures. I'm guessing either some of L-Pod or some Offshores. Anybody else have any insights about who it might have been? Rich Osborne

Tuesday, July 24, 2001 11:00  

<br/><b>Reported by:</b> 	Penny Stone	
<br/><b>Location:</b> 	Lummi Island	
<br/><b>Species:</b> 	Orca	
<br/><b>Pod:</b> 	L Pod, K Pod, J Pod	
<br/><b>Members:</b>
<br/><b>Direction:</b> 	south, toward Rosario Strait 	
 <br/><b>Comment:</b> These guys seem to be doing the round-the-islands shuffle lately - we had a big group off west side of Lummi Island today around 11:00 a.m. moving south pretty fast towards Rosario Strait - they were spread far out all across the strait - from in close to Lummi to east side of Orcas - looked like maybe J's and L's (maybe some K's?) - 2 big males and one looked like J1 from a distance. I'll be looking out for them Thursday..... Penny Stone

Monday, July 23, 2001 12:00  

<br/><b>Reported by:</b> 	Ron Bates, MMRG	
<br/><b>Location:</b> 	West side of San Juan Island	
<br/><b>Species:</b> 	Orca	
<br/><b>Pod:</b> 	L Pod, K Pod, J Pod	
<br/><b>Members:</b>
<br/><b>Direction:</b>
<br/><b>Comment:</b> Hi Susan A magic morning - had a few Jpod whales in the fog about 6 miles West of Hein Bank at 1144hrs, then K20 and K21 went by and in the next 15mins. the fog rolled away to reveal a superpod - J's K's and most of L's. Great vocals, spyhops and breaching. By the afternoon the fog was gone and the whales were spread from Eagle Pt. almost to Lime Kiln and out into the Strait itself (west side of San Juan Island). Ron Bates MMRG, Victoria

<br/><b>Reported by:</b> 	Paul Dee	
<br/><b>Location:</b> 	Mizen Head, Ireland	
<br/><b>Species:</b> 	Orca	
<br/><b>Pod:</b>
<br/><b>Members:</b>
<br/><b>Direction:</b>
<br/><b>Comment:</b> Hi there, Today at 4.14pm I was standing at the rails at the Mizen Head, the most south westerly point of Ireland and I spotted what I thought were dolphins approaching along the west coast. As they got closer I noticed that their movement was too lazy to be that of a dolphin. as they surfaced I recognized them as killer whales. One came up out of the water enough to let me see the distinctive black and white markings. I counted seven, a group of four and seconds later several hundred yards away a further three. They seemed to hang about near the Headland and I would guess that there were actually a lot more than seven judging by the distance between individual surfacing. I know there were 3 whales in Cork harbour lately and reported sightings several miles out to sea, but I'm wondering if there may be organisations interested in my sighting, or is it of any research value. Regards, Paul Dee.
