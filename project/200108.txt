<br/><b>Species:</b>	Orca	
<br/><b>Pod:</b>	L Pod	
<br/><b>Members:</b>	L-12 observed with a gash in her side	
<br/><b>Direction:</b>
<br/><b>Comments:</b>Hi Susan, I just got off the phone with the folks at Great Pacific Adventures. It seems that two of their drivers have seen L-12 (Alexis) with a 6-12 inch gash on her right side just below her saddle patch. The gash is healing. I had a private boater report that he saw a female orca on 8/5 with a 10 in gash on her left side just below her saddle patch. The gash appeared fresh. Just passing this info along to see if we have the same whale both times. Any one else got some info? Thanks Rowann

Friday, August 24, 2001 16:00 
<br/><b>Reported by:</b>	Sharyn Mellors	
<br/><b>Location:</b>	Admiralty Inlet, Whidbey Island	
<br/><b>Species:</b>	Orca	
<br/><b>Pod:</b>	Transient	
<br/><b>Members:</b>	3 - 4 orcas	
<br/><b>Direction:</b>	south 	
<br/><b>Comments:</b>Dear Susan, I am new at this so I was unable to count them or tell you what pod. But there are what appear to be orcas in admirals cove about a third of the way between ledgewood beach and the Point Wilson light house (4:20 pm). There seemed to be at least 3 or 4. I saw the last one at 5:05pm. The last time I saw any they were heading south. I have trees that keep me from seeing much in that direction. They seemed to stay in the same general area for most of the time I was watching. They were going in all directions. Maybe they were playing or perhaps eating but they were not traveling. I saw black fins going up and then down in the water in an arc. There seemed to be a couple of different sizes and I was able to count 3 or 4 fins that didn't seem to be attached to each other. I was using a telescope so I had a limited field of view at any one time. I hope that this information helps. Sharyn Mellors Ledgewood beach
